Math 7390 - Geometric Representation Theory

Instructor: Harrison Chen

Lecture: MWF 11:20-12:10, online
Office Hours: by appointment
E-mail: chenhi at cornell dot edu

We will cover topics in geometric representation theory. Namely, we will focus on three categories: equivariant, monodromic) D-modules on the flag variety, (equivariant, monodromic) perverse sheaves on the flag variety, and category O for a semisimple Lie algebra. These categories are related by Riemann-Hilbert and Beilinson-Bernstein. They share features such as minimal extensions, standard and costandard objects, projective covers and injective hulls, tilting objects. Other topics: Endomorphismensatz and Struktursatz, the Kazhdan-Lusztig conjectures.

Note: we may not get through all this, it's just a possible list of topics. Also open to various directions depending on interest. Update: We ended up covering just D-modules, category O, Beilinson-Bernstein, and touching on Riemann-Hilbert.

Syllabus:

Each Friday, students will take turns giving presentations. The idea is this will mostly be a worked example (or exercise), but there's a lot of flexibility. Sometimes this will be a full class, sometimes only half.

Furthermore, after every lecture each student must think of a question, or think of an answer to a question others might find confusing, and type-set it in the Overleaf project ("Q&A") below. All students are encouraged to participate, and help answer open questions.

The lecture notes will contain exercises.
